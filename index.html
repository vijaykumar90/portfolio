Research

Published & submitted work

Submitted · Knowledge-Based Systems

ConvexLDA: A Convex Formulation of Linear Discriminant Analysis

A fully convex, differentiable reformulation of LDA that replaces the ill-conditioned matrix inversion at the heart of classical LDA with a smooth log-determinant surrogate. Proven convex with O(1/t) convergence, and benchmarked against Fisher LDA and 11 state-of-the-art variants across 18 datasets spanning imaging, speech, and single-cell RNA-seq — outperforming the best prior method by 20–25 points on high-dimensional biological data.

Submitted · Knowledge-Based Systems

Generative AI in Biology: A Critical Review of Applications, Challenges, and Implications for High-Dimensional Omics Data

A systematic review of over 500 papers (2015–2024) mapping how GANs, VAEs, and Diffusion Models have been applied to high-dimensional Omics data, cataloging 128 biological datasets and their preprocessing strategies. Led the empirical benchmarking of VAE and scDiffusion architectures on three HDLSS microarray datasets (Breast Cancer, Motion Sickness, IBD), showing VAEs preserve distributional fidelity in small-sample regimes while GAN- and diffusion-based models require substantially more data to avoid mode collapse and sample degeneration.

Generative AIVAE Diffusion ModelsHDLSS

with Dr. Tomojit Ghosh, Wright State University, and collaborators at the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga

Preprint

Sample-Induced Block-wise DDPM for Synthetic Data Generation in HDLSS Regime

Vanilla diffusion models collapse when trained on high-dimensional, low-sample transcriptomic data. This work partitions the feature space into blocks, trains an independent DDPM per block, and reconstructs coherent synthetic samples via a sample-induced generation strategy — improving geometric fidelity over vanilla DDPM and scDiffusion, and measurably boosting downstream classification accuracy on real cancer and gene-expression datasets.
